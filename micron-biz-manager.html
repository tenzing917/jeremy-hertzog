Why I Want This Job

This role is about owning the business side of products across their full lifecycle, from planning through execution through end-of-life, and being accountable for results along the way. That's a pretty accurate description of how I've actually spent the last eight years, just in different industries and at different scales. I grew up in Boise and I'm moving back with my family, and CMBU is working on products at the center of the AI and cloud infrastructure shift, which is the kind of work that's hard to find outside of a handful of companies in the world.

Three Things I'd Tackle

Owning a business plan that delivers actual targets.

At Affinity, I helped drive the company from $17M to $60M ARR. That meant quarterly planning, demand forecasting, and constant alignment between product, sales, marketing, and operations. As a founder at RTW Flooring, I built business plans, set pricing, managed margins, and generated $550K in qualified pipeline in under five months. I've had my name next to a number and felt the weight of being the person responsible for whether it gets hit.

Being the hub that keeps cross-functional teams moving together.

Product launches at Affinity required tight coordination across engineering, sales, marketing, Customer Success, and senior leadership. Big product launches and keynote presentations meant aligning multiple departments and managing up through executives who cared deeply about the details. At Jelli, I was one of the first employees and wore every hat: Customer Success, enablement, product marketing, lifecycle. I've spent my career learning how different teams think and how to keep them rowing in the same direction.

Making portfolio tradeoff decisions with data and good judgment.

GTM timing, roadmap bets, and end-of-life calls all require both. My win/loss programs at Affinity gave product and sales teams the data they needed to make better decisions about what to build and how to sell it. But I've also learned to trust the pattern recognition that comes from sitting in hundreds of customer conversations and watching what actually moves a market versus what sounds good in a strategy deck.

Questions I'd Ask in My First Week
  • Where are the biggest gaps between current plans and where we need to be?
  • Where do cross-functional handoffs work well, and where do they break down?
  • What portfolio decisions are on the horizon in the next six months?
How I Think About This Work

Create clarity in complexity. Customers want different things. Products have tradeoffs. Internal teams have different incentives. Your job is to make it all work toward a shared outcome by asking: what does the data say, what does the customer need, what can we actually execute, and what's the highest-leverage move right now? I'm comfortable with ambiguity, but I always push toward a clear recommendation.

How I'd Want to Be Held Accountable

  • Portfolio health: margin performance, lifecycle management, GTM execution quality.
