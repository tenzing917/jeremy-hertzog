Three Things I'd Tackle

Creating content that makes a giant company feel personal.

Simplot spans food processing, mining, fertilizer, farming, and ranching across nine countries. The risk is content that reads like it was written for everyone and lands with no one. At Affinity, I wrote for audiences as different as junior analysts and Fortune 500 managing partners and made both groups feel like the message was for them. The trick is always specificity. Real stories about real people doing real work outperform corporate generalities every single time.

Building editorial standards that enable good work, not slow it down.

At Affinity, I created the messaging frameworks that became the source of truth for how the company described itself, its products, and its value. At RTW Flooring, I built the entire brand identity from scratch: name, voice, visual direction, website copy, social presence, sales pitch language, even the in-store experience. I've built these systems from zero and I know the difference between guardrails that help people do better work and bureaucracy that just creates bottlenecks.

Keeping content projects moving across teams.

The role coordinates with Creative Services, PR, Communications Business Partners, and leadership. That's project management muscle I've built across every company I've worked at. I've managed agencies and offshore talent on projects, coordinated big product launches with tight cross-functional dependencies, and produced keynote presentations that required alignment through senior leadership. I keep things organized, I flag problems before they become emergencies, and I respect other people's time.

Questions I'd Ask in My First Week
  • What does the editorial workflow look like end-to-end, and where does it typically slow down?
  • Which channels are working well and which feel like they're going through the motions?
  • What does the Simplot voice sound like at its best, and where does it tend to get muddled?
How I Think About This Work

The core brand story stays the same, but how you tell it has to shift by audience, channel, and moment. And the best brand content almost never comes from the comms team brainstorming in a conference room. It comes from going out into the company, finding the people doing interesting work, and helping them tell their own story in a way that reflects who Simplot is and what it stands for.

How I'd Want to Be Held Accountable
  • Content quality and brand consistency across everything that goes out.
  • Projects delivered on time and on brief.
  • Whether content actually performs in terms of reach, engagement, and relevance.
  • Whether internal partners find the editorial process easy and productive to work with.
