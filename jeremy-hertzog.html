Problems I'm Built to Solve

Your product is good but nobody can explain it clearly.

I build messaging that connects what a company does to why anyone should care — and then turn it into materials people actually use. At Affinity, I built the messaging architecture the whole company adopted, from the boardroom to individual sales calls. At Autodesk, I translated construction risk scoring into language that four different user types could each connect with. At RTW Flooring, I built an entire brand and sales pitch from nothing — $550K in pipeline in under five months.

Your sales team is losing deals and nobody knows why.

At Affinity, I built the win/loss program from scratch — structured interviews, deal-level analysis, pattern identification. That program changed how product prioritized what to build and how sales positioned against competitors. I turned those insights into battlecards, talk tracks, and competitive comparisons that directly shortened sales cycles and improved win rates. I also ran continuous competitive intelligence and customer advisory boards to keep those materials current.

Your organization is going through change and people are confused.

At Jelli, I was employee #25 when we were acquired by iHeartMedia — I helped shape how the combined organization communicated through a completely new identity and structure. At Affinity, I led communication strategy through multiple reorgs, leadership transitions, and shifting strategy as the company scaled from $17M to $60M ARR. At RTW Flooring, I brought a small team through constant pivots with no playbook. In every case, the work was the same: make sure people understand what's happening, why, and what it means for them.

Your teams are working hard but not working together.

At Affinity, product launches required coordinating across engineering, sales, marketing, Customer Success, and senior leadership. I managed 4 direct reports, agencies, and offshore talent on top of that. At Jelli, I wore every hat — Customer Success, partner marketing, sales enablement, product marketing, content, solution engineering, AdWords — because the company needed someone who could work across every function. I know how different teams think and what it takes to keep them aligned.
