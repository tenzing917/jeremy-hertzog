Why I Want This Job

I grew up in Boise and I'm moving back with my wife and family. When I saw Tuff Jug was hiring for this role, two things clicked immediately. First, this is a product company that actually makes something, manufactured right in Nampa, solving a real problem that anyone who's used a standard jug understands viscerally. I've spent years marketing software that's hard to explain; the idea of marketing a product people can hold, use, and instantly understand is genuinely appealing. Second, building a DTC growth engine from scratch is exactly what I've been doing across my last three roles, just in different contexts. The challenge of turning a great physical product into a brand people seek out online, that's the kind of problem I want to work on.

Three Things I'd Tackle

Building a customer acquisition engine that actually scales.

At Affinity, I helped build the growth engine that scaled the company from $17M to $60M in ARR. At Right This Way Flooring, I built the entire demand generation system from zero, producing $550K in qualified pipeline in under five months. In both cases, I started by understanding who the customer actually was, what channels they lived in, and what message would move them, then built systematic acquisition motions around those insights. For Tuff Jug, the DTC opportunity is wide open because the product basically sells itself once someone sees it work. The challenge is getting it in front of the right people at the right time, and that's what I do.

Turning performance data into better decisions, not just better dashboards.

I've built analytics infrastructure at every company I've worked at. At Affinity, I partnered with our Head of Competitive Intelligence to build a structured win/loss program, and I was the person who turned those insights into pricing decisions, competitive positioning, demand gen strategy, and content. At Right This Way Flooring, I tracked every lead source, conversion rate, and cost-per-acquisition to know exactly where to put the next dollar. I don't just look at KPIs; I use them to make decisions about where to invest and where to cut. For a DTC brand, that means knowing which channels are actually driving profitable revenue and which are just generating vanity metrics.

Cross-functional leadership when everyone's wearing multiple hats.

At Jelli, I was employee number 25, wearing every hat from Customer Success to product marketing to demand gen. At Right This Way Flooring, I co-founded the business, which meant I was the marketing team, the sales team, and the operations team simultaneously. I've also managed teams of four and coordinated across product, engineering, sales, and Customer Success at companies like Affinity and Autodesk. At a company like Tuff Jug where the team is small and moving fast, you need someone who can think strategically and also do the work. That's how I've operated my entire career.

Questions I'd Ask in My First Week
  • What does the current customer acquisition funnel look like, and where are the biggest drop-off points?
  • Which channels have you tested so far, and what's worked versus what hasn't?
  • How are you currently thinking about the balance between DTC and retail/dealer channel growth?
  • What does the tech stack look like today for eCommerce, analytics, and marketing automation?
  • Who is the core customer right now, and is there a segment you think you're missing?
How I Think About This Work
Growth isn't just about running ads. It's about understanding why someone buys, building systems that put your product in front of more people like them, and then measuring what actually worked so you can do more of it. The companies I've helped grow didn't get there by guessing. They got there by building a feedback loop between customer insight, channel strategy, and performance data, and then iterating relentlessly.
How I'd Want to Be Held Accountable
  • Revenue growth and customer acquisition cost tracked monthly against targets
  • Channel-level performance transparency with clear attribution
  • Pipeline and conversion metrics visible to the whole leadership team
  • Honest reporting on what's working and what's not, with recommendations, not just numbers
  • Quarterly strategic reviews that connect marketing activity to business outcomes
