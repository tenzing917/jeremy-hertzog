Three Things I'd Tackle

Building documentation and training that keeps pace with expansion.

When Affinity scaled, I was the one creating the enablement infrastructure: battlecards, pitch decks, onboarding materials, customer case studies formatted as pain/solution/metric. These weren't one-off documents. They were systems with templates, standards, and update cycles. I understand what it takes to build knowledge resources that people actually use, not just files that sit in a shared drive collecting dust.

Making technical content land for the people who need it.

I've spent my career translating expert-level information for non-expert audiences. At Autodesk, I took construction risk scoring methodology and turned it into materials that sales reps and customer success managers could confidently present to clients. At Jelli, I did customer onboarding, implementation support, and solution engineering, which meant creating step-by-step materials for people encountering a brand new platform for the first time.

Connecting documentation to real performance outcomes.

At Affinity, my win/loss data fed directly back into enablement materials. When reps lost deals for specific reasons, I updated the battlecards. When customers churned for identifiable gaps, I updated the onboarding content. Documentation should be a living system that gets sharper based on what's actually happening in the field, not a static archive that nobody revisits.

Questions I'd Ask in My First Week
  • What does the documentation ecosystem look like right now, and where are the biggest gaps?
  • How do subject matter experts contribute, and what slows them down?
  • What's the expansion timeline, and what does the training ramp need to look like?
How I Think About This Work

Start from the moment of need. When someone needs this information, where are they? What are they doing? How much time do they have? Build the format and delivery around that reality. Use templates and standards for consistency, but treat the whole system as something that should improve every quarter based on what the people using it are telling you.

How I'd Want to Be Held Accountable
  • Whether documentation is accurate and current during expansion milestones.
  • Training completion rates and time-to-competency for new team members.
  • Whether SMEs find the process easy to work with.
  • System adoption and usage.
